Insurance Adequacy Calculator

How much insurance cover should you have?

This insurance adequacy calculator is designed to help you discuss with your adviser the ideal level of cover that you should have to protect you and your family in the event of an illness or accident to provide you the financial assistance necessary.

Our calculator will look at a number of scenarios including selling down assets to provide some cover that will complement the cover that you need.

We also consider whether you should have the cover inside your superannuation fund or paid by you directly. This calculator will generate a general report but should not be considered advice. We encourage you to appoint our professional adviser to help you with selecting the best insurer and arranging the cover and managing any claims that may rise.

You can consider a whole range of scenarios and what if’s.

Assumptions

  1. Am I locked into the cover for a certain period?
      2. No. The policy that you establish can be cancelled at any time without any penalties.
  2. Can the Insurance Company cancel my policy due to my health?
      3. No, the insurance company cannot cancel your policy due to a change in your health. (Unless you fail to disclose details at the application stage)
  3. Am I covered whilst travelling overseas?
      4. Yes, all of our insurance products provide cover 24 hours a day, wherever you are in the world.
  4. How do the different policies you have quoted compare?
      5. We aim to provide clients with as much choice as possible and show the premiums of various companies for various levels of cover. With term life insurance is it purely cost based, as all companies will pay when you die. Trauma and Income Protection policies will vary between companies. We can provide specific comparisons if you wish.
  5. Does the policy have a cash value?
      6. The only policies that have a cash value are older style whole of life policies or a money-back life policy. These are generally very expensive and only a couple of companies still offer them. What we have quoted is term life cover, which does not have a cash value.
  6. How do I pay my premiums?
      7. Companies offer the choice of 3 methods of payment (monthly, half yearly or annually): • Direct debit • Credit card • Cheque (not available for monthly)
  7. Do I need to have a Medical examination before taking out a policy?
      8. Most people do not require a medical for establishment of cover. Whether you will need any blood test or medical requirements will be determined by your age and the amount of cover that you apply for. The life company will pay for any medical requirements and we arrange these for you. In most cases we can send a nurse to see you to complete the tests if the company requires you to have them. That way the process is as quick and simple as possible.
  8. Are there any tax implications?
      9. If a Term Life policy is written through a superannuation fund, the premiums are a tax Deduction to the superannuation fund. Taxation of death benefits depends on whether the benefits are paid to a dependent, non-dependent or to the Trustee of the deceased's estate, and the total amount in superannuation. If a Term Life policy is not written through a superannuation fund, the premiums are not tax deductible and any proceeds recovered are not considered to be assessable income. However, everyone's circumstances are different, and we are not tax advisers. You should seek taxation advice from an independent tax adviser.
